# Walk as Children of Light

**Series:** Ephesians: Our Riches in Christ
**Text:** Ephesians 5:3–14
**Length:** ~15 minutes

---

## Introduction

Let me ask you something. Have you ever walked into a room and immediately felt something was *off*?

Maybe it's a kitchen where someone left a rotten apple in the fruit bowl. You don't have to inspect every piece — you just know. Something doesn't belong.

Or maybe it's a clean, well-lit room — and then someone walks in and turns on a single extra light. Suddenly you notice the dust on the shelves, the smudge on the window, the crack in the corner you never saw before. The room didn't change. But the light changed everything about how you see it.

This is where Paul takes us this morning.

If you remember, we've been walking through Ephesians — three chapters of breathtaking grace. Chosen before the foundation of the world. Adopted as sons and daughters. Saved by grace through faith, not of works.

Last week we saw Paul's first "therefore" — *"Therefore, be imitators of God, as beloved children. And walk in love."*

This week he keeps turning the same corner. But now he gets more specific. Because if you're a child of God — if the light of Christ is actually in you — then there are things that simply *don't belong* in your life anymore. And there are things that *do*.

Let's read it together.

> **Ephesians 5:3–14 (ESV)**
>
> *But sexual immorality and all impurity or covetousness must not even be named among you, as is proper among saints. Let there be no filthiness nor foolish talk nor crude joking, which are out of place, but instead let there be thanksgiving. For you may be sure of this, that everyone who is sexually immoral or impure, or who is covetous (that is, an idolater), has no inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and God. Let no one deceive you with empty words, for because of these things the wrath of God comes upon the sons of disobedience. Therefore do not become partners with them.*
>
> *For at one time you were darkness, but now you are light in the Lord. Walk as children of light (for the fruit of light is found in all that is good and right and true), and try to discern what is pleasing to the Lord. Take no part in the unfruitful works of darkness, but instead expose them. For it is shameful even to speak of the things that they do in secret. But when anything is exposed by the light, it becomes visible, for anything that becomes visible is light. Therefore it says,*
>
> *"Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you."*

Two movements this morning: what light **rejects**, and what light **does**.

---

## Point 1: The Put-Off — What Light Rejects (vv. 3–7)

Look at verse 3: *"But sexual immorality and all impurity or covetousness must not even be named among you, as is proper among saints."*

Paul doesn't just say avoid these things. He says they should not even be *named* among you. That's a striking phrase. He's not saying pretend the world doesn't exist. He's saying these things should have no reputation, no comfortable familiarity, no foothold in the family of God.

And he gives us a three-part list:

**Deeds** — sexual immorality, impurity, greed. Things you do.
**Speech** — filthiness, foolish talk, crude joking. Things you say.
**People** — the sexually immoral, the impure, the covetous. The company you keep.

Paul is covering every angle. Not just actions — but words. Not just words — but what you let become normal.

But here's where it gets interesting. Look at verse 4 again: *"Let there be no filthiness nor foolish talk nor crude joking, which are out of place, **but instead let there be thanksgiving.** "*

Paul doesn't just say "stop." He gives you a replacement. Instead of filthy speech — **thanksgiving**. Instead of greed — **gratitude**.

Think about it. A heart that is genuinely thankful for what God has given doesn't crave what it shouldn't take. A mouth that is busy thanking God doesn't have room for crude talk. Gratitude is the antidote because gratitude turns your eyes toward what you already have — and when you see that, the cheap substitutes lose their appeal.

And then the warning in verses 5–6: *"Let no one deceive you with empty words."*

Someone will tell you it doesn't matter. Everyone does it. It's harmless. Paul says: do not be deceived. The world will call evil good. But light calls it what it is.

**Application:** Here's the question only you can answer this morning. *Is there anything in your life — a habit, a relationship, a conversation pattern, something you watch or click or laugh at — that you know doesn't belong there, but you've been making excuses for?* Paul says: don't just cover it up. Don't just hide it. Remove it entirely. Because it has no place among the saints. Not because you're earning God's favor — but because you already *have* it, and light and darkness don't mix.

---

## Point 2: The Put-On — What Light Does (vv. 8–14)

Now Paul hits the gospel engine. And this is the most important sentence in the whole passage. Verse 8:

*"For at one time you were darkness, but now you are light in the Lord. Walk as children of light."*

Notice carefully what Paul does not say. He does not say: "You were *in* darkness, and now you're *in* light." That would just be relocation — a change of address.

He says: **You *were* darkness. And now you *are* light.**

That's a change of identity. A change of nature. You are not just someone who *has* the light. You *are* light because the Light of the world lives in you.

And this is exactly the same truth Paul gave us in verse 1 of this chapter, when he said: *"Be imitators of God, as beloved children."* A child doesn't imitate to *become* a child. A child imitates because they already *are* one.

Same logic here. You don't clean up your life to *become* light. You *are* light. So clean up.

And what does light produce? Verse 9: *"The fruit of light is found in all that is good and right and true."*

Goodness — doing the good works God prepared for you.
Righteousness — right relationships, right conduct.
Truth — not just speaking truth, but *living* truth.

And verse 10 — you're not just following rules. You're learning to discern *what pleases the Lord.* That's a relationship, not a rulebook.

Then verses 11–14 give us the second half of what light does. It doesn't just purify — it **exposes**.

*"Take no part in the unfruitful works of darkness, but instead expose them."*

How does light expose darkness? Not by going looking for it with a megaphone. Not by yelling at people. Light exposes by simply *being present*.

Turn on a light in a dark room — you don't have to chase the cockroaches. They scatter. Not because the light attacked them. Because the light is there.

That's how your life works when you walk as a child of light. You don't have to go find darkness to rebuke. Just let your life shine, and the contrast does the work.

And the goal isn't condemnation. Look at verse 13: *"When anything is exposed by the light, it becomes visible, for anything that becomes visible is light."*

The same light that reveals sin can also produce **salvation**. People see the difference in you, and they're drawn to the Source.

Paul closes with what sounds like an early baptismal hymn: *"Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you."*

Some of you have fallen asleep. Not in the sense of losing your salvation — but in the sense of drifting. You've let the world's dim light become normal. You've let things slide that used to matter. This is your wake-up call.

**Application:** Here's the test. *Is your life bright enough that someone walking in darkness notices the difference?* Not because you're perfect. But because the light of Christ is really in you. The best evangelism isn't a script — it's a life so different that people want to know why.

---

## Conclusion

Remember that clean, well-lit room I mentioned at the start? When you turn on that extra light, the dust doesn't just disappear. It becomes *visible*. And once you see it, you can clean it.

That's what Paul is calling us to this morning. Not to hide in the dark, pretending the dust isn't there. But to step fully into the light — to let God's light expose what needs to go, and then to walk as the children of light we already are.

Here's the question that decides everything:

**Is there an area of your life where you've been living in dim light — telling yourself it's fine, it's normal, everyone does it — when the Spirit has been gently saying, "That doesn't belong here"?**

If you're a believer this morning, the answer isn't "try harder." The answer is: *You are light. Now live like it.* Let the gratitude replace the greed. Let the goodness replace the filth. Let your life shine bright enough that someone else might wake up and come to the light.

And if you're here and you've never stepped into the light at all — you're still walking in darkness, still trying to find your own way — then hear these words: *Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you.*

He is the light of the world. And if you follow Him, you will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life.

*Let's pray.*

> Father, thank You that we were darkness, but now we are light in You. Lord, show us the things in our lives that don't belong — not to shame us, but to free us. Give us grateful hearts that don't crave what isn't ours. Let our lives shine so brightly that the darkness around us has no choice but to retreat. And for anyone still sleeping this morning — still walking in the dark — let this be the moment they hear Your voice: Wake up. Arise. Christ is shining on you. In Jesus' name, Amen.
