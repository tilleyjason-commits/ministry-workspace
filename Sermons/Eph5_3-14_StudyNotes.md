# Ephesians 5:3–14 — Sermon Study Notes

**Series:** Ephesians: Our Riches in Christ
**Passage:** Ephesians 5:3–14
**Theme:** Walk as Children of Light

---

## Overview: The Big Picture

This passage is one continuous argument with three movements:

> **Abstain from impurity (vv. 3–7) → Walk as light (vv. 8–10) → Expose darkness (vv. 11–14)**

The connective tissue is **identity**: because of who you ARE in Christ, here's how you LIVE. The same engine that drove 5:1–2 — "beloved children" → "walk in love" — now drives 5:3–14: "light in the Lord" → "walk as children of light."

### Where We Are in the Series
- Chs. 1–3: The doctrinal mountain (chosen, adopted, sealed, saved, fellow citizens)
- Eph 5:1–2 (preached): "Beloved children → imitate the Father → walk in love"
- Eph 5:3–14 (next): "You were darkness, now you are light → walk as light → expose darkness"

## Movement 1 — vv. 3–7: Abstain from Impurity

### The "Not Even Named" List

Paul gives three trios of sin:

| Category | Sin Trio |
|----------|----------|
| **Deeds** (v. 3) | Sexual immorality, impurity, greed |
| **Speech** (v. 4) | Filthiness, silly talk, crude joking |
| **People** (v. 5) | The immoral, the impure, the covetous (idolater) |

### Key Exegetical Insights

**1. "Must not even be named"** (v. 3) — Paul's language is emphatic. These things don't just need to be avoided; they shouldn't even have a reputation among believers. Not that we pretend they don't exist, but that they have no place, no foothold, no comfortable familiarity in the church.

**2. Greed = idolatry** (v. 5) — Paul connects sexual lust with greed because both elevate a created thing to the center of life — the place only God belongs. The covetous person is an *idolater* not because he worships a statue but because a desire has become a god.

**3. The replacement is thanksgiving** (v. 4) — Paul doesn't just say "stop that" — he gives a replacement. Instead of filthy speech → gratitude. Instead of greed → a heart that thanks God for what it has. A thankful person isn't grasping for what they shouldn't take.

**4. "Empty words"** (v. 6) — Paul warns against false prophets who call evil "good" or minimize sin. The world will tell you these things are normal, harmless, even healthy. Don't be deceived.

**5. "Do not be partakers"** (v. 7) — This isn't a call to isolate from unbelievers; it's a call to separate from the *lifestyle*. You can love the lost without adopting their values.

### Application Angle

The question each believer must face: *Is there anything in my life that I know doesn't belong there but I've been making excuses for?* Paul says: don't just hide it — remove it entirely, because it has no place among the saints.

## Movement 2 — vv. 8–10: Walk as Children of Light

### The Pivot Point (v. 8)

> *"For at one time you were darkness, but now you are light in the Lord."*

This is the **gospel engine** of the entire passage. Notice Paul doesn't say:
- "You were *in* darkness, now you're *in* light" (relocation)
- He says you **were** darkness, and now you **are** light (identity transformation)

This echoes what you already preached in 5:1–2: a child doesn't imitate to *become* a child — a child imitates because they *already are* one. Same logic here: you don't clean up to *become* light — you *are* light, so you clean up.

### The Fruit of Light (v. 9)

Three qualities:
1. **All goodness** — doing the good works God prepared (2:10)
2. **Righteousness** — right relationships, right conduct
3. **Truth** — not just spoken truth, but *lived* truth (cf. 4:15, "speaking the truth in love")

### The Discernment Call (v. 10)

> *"Trying to learn what is pleasing to the Lord."*

The Greek word *dokimazō* means "testing" — like a metalworker testing metal to see if it's genuine. It's an active, ongoing process of forming Christian judgment. The Christian life isn't a rulebook to memorize — it's a relationship that teaches you what pleases the Father.

### Application Angle

Ask yourself: *Am I living like someone whose identity has actually changed? Or am I still functioning out of my old nature?* The Christian life is not about trying harder — it's about becoming who you already are in Christ.

## Movement 3 — vv. 11–14: Expose the Darkness

### Two-Sided Command (v. 11)

| Negative | Positive |
|----------|----------|
| Don't participate in unfruitful deeds of darkness | Instead, expose them |

**Key insight:** "Unfruitful" — darkness produces nothing of lasting value. The world's pleasures promise fruit but deliver emptiness.

### How Does Light Expose?

The Greek word *elenchō* can mean:
1. To expose, convict, bring to light
2. To reprove, rebuke

**Primary meaning in context:** The most powerful exposure of darkness is a life that's *visibly different*. A believer doesn't have to go looking for darkness to rebuke — just let your life shine, and the contrast does the work.

### Three Stages of Grace (v. 13)

Francis Foulkes outlines the progression:
1. People are exposed by the light
2. They allow themselves to be revealed
3. They *become* light

Light doesn't just expose darkness — it *transforms* it. The same light that reveals sin can also produce salvation.

### The Early Christian Hymn (v. 14)

> *"Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you."*

This is likely an early baptismal hymn — words sung when new believers emerged from the waters. Two valid interpretations:

1. **Primary context:** A wake-up call to believers who have fallen asleep in sin (cf. Romans 13:11; 1 Thessalonians 5:6)
2. **Secondary context:** An evangelistic call — the light of your life awakens the spiritually dead around you

Both readings reinforce each other, but the immediate context points to Christians who need to wake up.

### Application Angle

*Is your life bright enough that someone walking in darkness notices the difference?* The best evangelism isn't a script — it's a life so different that people want to know why.

## Structural Options for the Sermon

### Big Idea
> Because you are light in the Lord, live like it — leave the darkness behind, and let your life expose what's false.

### Option A — Three Movements (follows the text)

| Point | Verses | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **I. What Light Rejects** | 3–7 | Purity matters because of who you are |
| **II. What Light Produces** | 8–10 | Goodness, righteousness, truth |
| **III. What Light Does** | 11–14 | Exposes darkness and awakens sleepers |

### Option B — Two Movements (simpler)

| Point | Verses | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **I. The Put-Off** | 3–7 | What doesn't belong in your life anymore |
| **II. The Put-On** | 8–14 | Walk as light — expose darkness by your life |

---

## Key Themes to Weave Through

1. **Identity drives behavior** — "You WERE darkness, NOW you ARE light" is the gospel center every point must flow from
2. **Gratitude is the antidote** — Filthy speech → thanksgiving. Greed → gratitude. The heart that thanks God doesn't crave what it shouldn't take
3. **Light exposes by being present** — You don't need a megaphone; just let the contrast of your life do its work
4. **Awakening** — Some believers have fallen asleep. The hymn in v. 14 calls us back to alertness
5. **Grace precedes command** — As always: identity first, then ethics. Never reverse the order

## Potential Illustration Ideas

1. **What doesn't belong (vv. 3–4):** A clean kitchen vs. a kitchen with one rotten apple on the counter — you don't just cover it with a dish towel, you throw it out because it doesn't belong where you live and eat
2. **Gratitude replaces greed (v. 4):** A child at a birthday party who keeps receiving gifts vs. a child sneaking cookies from the jar — the first is satisfied, the second is always craving more
3. **You were darkness, now you are light (v. 8):** A room that was pitch black — then someone flips the switch. The room didn't *try* to be light; the light was turned on. Same with us in Christ
4. **Light exposes (v. 11):** Turn on a light in a dark room — cockroaches scatter. Not because you yelled at them, but because the light is there. Your life works the same way
5. **"Awake, sleeper" (v. 14):** A smoke alarm at 3 AM — jarring, uncomfortable, but it saves your life
