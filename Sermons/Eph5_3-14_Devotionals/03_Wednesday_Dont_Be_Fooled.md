# Wednesday — Don't Be Fooled

**Scripture Focus:** Ephesians 5:5–7

> *"For you may be sure of this, that everyone who is sexually immoral or impure, or who is covetous (that is, an idolater), has no inheritance in the kingdom of Christ and God. Let no one deceive you with empty words, for because of these things the wrath of God comes upon the sons of disobedience. Therefore do not become partners with them."*

---

Today's passage is heavier. I'm not going to apologize for that, because you're old enough to handle the truth, and I'd rather you hear it from someone who loves you than figure it out the hard way.

Paul says three things here that you need to tattoo on your heart:

**One:** The people who live in this stuff — who make it their lifestyle, not just a struggle but a settled pattern — don't belong to the kingdom. That's not me being harsh. That's Paul. He says "you may be sure of this." No maybe. No "well, it depends." If your life is defined by sexual sin, impurity, and wanting what you shouldn't have — you're not living like someone who belongs to Jesus.

**Two:** Somebody is going to try to convince you that's not true. Paul says "let no one deceive you with empty words." Somebody — a friend, a show you watch, a voice on the internet, maybe even a teacher — is going to tell you that sin is normal, that everyone does it, that God doesn't really care, that your body is your business and nobody else gets a say. Those are empty words. They sound wise, but they're hollow. They have no weight on judgment day.

**Three:** Don't partner with them. This doesn't mean you can't be friends with people who don't know Jesus. Jesus ate with sinners. But it means you don't join their program. You don't adopt their values to fit in. You don't let them set the standard for what's normal.

---

## Relate It

You know how when you're trying to study and someone's blasting music in the next room? You can't concentrate. The noise gets into your head and messes with your focus.

That's what "empty words" do to your soul. The world is blasting a playlist of lies all day, every day:
- "It's your life, do what you want."
- "Nobody gets to tell you what to do with your body."
- "If it feels good, it can't be wrong."
- "Everybody's doing it. You'll be left out."

These sound convincing when you hear them 50 times a day. But they're empty. They're loud, they're confident, and they're lies. And Paul is saying: don't let them fool you. The room is full of noise, but that doesn't make it true.

---

## Illustrate It

Have you ever seen those videos where somebody hands a stranger a $20 bill, then says, "Actually, I changed my mind — I'll give you $100 if you let me slap you in the face first"? And some people take the deal. They take the slap for the money.

That's what the world is offering you. It says, "You can have the pleasure — just take the consequences too." A few minutes of fun, a little acceptance from the crowd, a reputation for being cool — and in exchange, you trade your peace, your purity, your closeness with God, maybe even your eternity.

Paul says the deal is a ripoff. Don't take it.

---

## Apply It

Here's your question today — and this one's tougher:

**Whose voice is louder in your life right now — God's or the world's?**

Who are you letting set the standard for what's normal? Your friends? Social media? The shows you watch? Or what God says in His Word?

I'm not asking you to become a monk. I'm not asking you to have no friends. I'm asking you to check who's influencing you more. Because you *will* become like the people you spend the most time with.

If your closest friends don't care about following Jesus, guess what's going to happen to your walk with Him?

You don't have to dump your friends. But you *do* have to decide whose team you're on. And once you know that, start finding some people who are on the same team.

---

## Prayer for Today

*Lord, protect me from the empty words of the world. Help me know Your voice when I hear it and recognize the lies when they come. Surround me with people who help me follow You, not pull me away. Amen.*
