# Friday — Wake Up, Your Life Matters

**Scripture Focus:** Ephesians 5:11–14

> *"Take no part in the unfruitful works of darkness, but instead expose them. For it is shameful even to speak of the things that they do in secret. But when anything is exposed by the light, it becomes visible, for anything that becomes visible is light. Therefore it says, 'Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you.'"*

---

Last one of the week. I saved a big one for Friday.

Paul ends this section with a picture that still gives me chills. He says the light doesn't just expose darkness — it *transforms* it. When light hits something dark, that thing becomes visible. And when something becomes visible, it has the chance to *become light itself.*

Then he quotes an early Christian hymn — words they probably sang at baptisms when new believers came up out of the water:

> *"Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you."*

Here's what Paul is saying with all of this:

**One:** Don't join the darkness. You're light now — stay out of the shadows.

**Two:** Don't just avoid the darkness. *Expose* it. Not by going around pointing fingers at everybody. No. By *being light*. Your life — the way you talk, the way you treat people, the things you say no to — exposes the darkness around you just by being there. Like turning on a light in a dirty room. The roaches don't scatter because you yelled at them. They scatter because the light is on.

**Three:** Some of you are asleep. The hymn is a wake-up call. Maybe you've been coasting. Maybe you've been going through the motions. Maybe you started strong but somewhere along the way you drifted off. Paul says: Open your eyes. Get up. Let Christ shine on you again.

---

## Relate It

You ever oversleep on a school day? That moment when your eyes snap open and you see the clock and you know you're late? There's that jolt of panic, the scramble, the "why didn't my alarm go off??"

Paul's wake-up call is like that — but for your soul. Some Christians are spiritually asleep. They're still coming to church, still singing the songs, still saying the right words. But they're on autopilot. Their heart isn't in it. They're not really *awake* to what God is doing.

Maybe that's you. Not in a scary, judgmental way — just honestly. Are you fully awake to Jesus right now? Or are you just going through the motions?

---

## Illustrate It

There's a story about a guy who was in a theater fire. The place was filling with smoke, people were panicking, and one man just sat in his seat. Someone yelled, "Get up! The building's on fire!" The man looked up, blinked, and said, "I'm fine. I'll catch the next show." He was in total denial. He couldn't smell the smoke anymore because he'd been sitting in it too long.

That's what spiritual sleep looks like. You don't realize how far you've drifted because you adjusted to the smell. You're still in the building, but the fire is real and you don't even notice.

The call from verse 14 is an alarm going off: **WAKE UP.** Not because God is angry. Because He loves you and doesn't want you to miss what He's doing.

---

## Apply It

Here's your Friday challenge — maybe the most important one of the week:

**Look at your life this past week. Are you awake? Or are you coasting?**

Ask yourself:
- Have I been praying like I actually believe God is listening?
- Have I opened my Bible this week because I wanted to hear from Him, or just because I was supposed to?
- Is there a sin I've made peace with that I need to wake up from?

If you're honest and you realize you've been asleep, here's the good news: this verse isn't a threat. It's an invitation. **"Christ will shine on you."** He's not standing over you with a disappointed look. He's standing there with light in His hands, ready to wake you up and fill your life with His presence again.

You don't have to stay asleep. Open your eyes. Get up. Let His light hit you.

And then — go be light for someone else. Your life matters more than you know. Some kid in your school, some kid on your team, some person in your life is walking in darkness. And you might be the only light they ever see.

Don't dim it. Let it shine.
