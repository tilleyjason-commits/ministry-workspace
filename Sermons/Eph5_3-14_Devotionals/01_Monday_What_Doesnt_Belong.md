# Monday — What Doesn't Belong

**Scripture Focus:** Ephesians 5:3–4a

> *"But sexual immorality and all impurity or covetousness must not even be named among you, as is proper among saints. Let there be no filthiness nor foolish talk nor crude joking, which are out of place."*

---

Hey man, pull up a chair. Grab a drink if you want. I want to talk with you about something that's going to come at you from every direction for the rest of your life — so I'd rather you hear it from me first, straight up, from the Bible.

Ever had someone hand you something and say, "Here, hold this for me"? Your hands are full, you don't really want it, but you take it anyway out of politeness? And then suddenly you're stuck carrying something you never asked for?

**What Paul is saying here is basically: don't even pick up what doesn't belong to you.**

He lists a few things: sexual immorality (anything God says is off-limits with your body), impurity (letting your mind wander where it shouldn't), greed (wanting more than what you have, especially when it comes to looking at people the wrong way). Then he adds the talk that goes with it — the crude jokes, the trash talk, the stuff you say with your friends that you'd never say in front of your mom.

And Paul says: "Must not even be **named** among you." He's not saying, "If you struggle with this, you're out." He's saying these things shouldn't get comfortable in your life. They shouldn't have a nickname, a regular seat at your table, a shortcut on your phone. They don't belong.

---

## Relate It

Think about your phone for a second. You've got apps on there, right? Some you use every day. Some you downloaded once and forgot about. Some you keep because everyone else has them.

Now imagine someone handed you a shirt that had something nasty written across it. Would you wear it? Probably not. You'd say "that's not me."

Same thing with what goes into your eyes, your ears, your mouth. The apps you scroll. The jokes you laugh at. The videos that pop up. The things you type in a search bar when nobody's watching. If it doesn't belong on a child of God, don't let it get comfortable in your life.

You're not a trash can. Stop letting people throw their garbage into your soul.

---

## Illustrate It

When I was about your age, I had a buddy who kept a half-eaten sandwich under his bed. I'm not kidding. He'd take a bite, wrap it up, shove it under there, and grab it again the next day. After a few days, his room started smelling. He didn't even notice anymore because he'd gotten used to it. But everyone who walked in noticed immediately.

That's what sin does. It starts small — a little thing you hold onto, a joke you repeat, a video you watch "just once." You get used to it. You don't even smell it anymore. But it's still rotting in there, and it's changing the air you breathe.

Paul says: don't let it in the room. Not even under the bed.

---

## Apply It

Here's your question for today — and don't just answer it in your head. Actually think about it:

**Is there anything in my life that I know doesn't belong there, but I've been making excuses for?**

Maybe it's an app. A group chat. A show you watch. A way you talk when your parents aren't in the room. Something you'd be embarrassed to have Jesus walk in on.

If it doesn't belong, don't just hide it. Don't just feel guilty about it. **Remove it.** Because you're not a fixer-upper project — you're a saint. That means you're set apart. Set apart means "not like everybody else."

And that's a good thing.

---

## Prayer for Today

*Father, thank You that You've called me Your child and set me apart for something better. Show me today anything in my life that doesn't belong there — and give me the guts to get rid of it. Amen.*
