# Thursday — You Are Light

**Scripture Focus:** Ephesians 5:8–10

> *"For at one time you were darkness, but now you are light in the Lord. Walk as children of light (for the fruit of light is found in all that is good and right and true), and try to discern what is pleasing to the Lord."*

---

This is my favorite verse in this whole passage. I mean it. If you only remember one thing from this week, let it be **verse 8**.

Here's why: Paul doesn't say "you used to *live in* darkness and now you *live in* light." He says you **were** darkness. Past tense. That was your identity. And now you **are** light. That's your new identity.

This is not about where you live. It's about **who you are**.

Think about it. A lightbulb doesn't try to be bright. It just is. When you flip the switch, the bulb doesn't say, "Okay, I need to really focus now and produce some photons." No. It has power running through it, and light comes out naturally.

That's you. If you belong to Jesus, you have His Spirit in you. And light comes out naturally when the Spirit is flowing. You don't have to force it. You just have to *be who you are*.

Then Paul gives you the fruit — the evidence that light is really there. Three things:
- **Good** — doing the right thing, even when nobody's watching
- **Right** — treating people the way God says to treat them
- **True** — living honestly, not pretending to be something you're not

And then verse 10 gives you the process: *"Try to discern what is pleasing to the Lord."* That word "discern" means to test, to check, to figure out. It's like when you're cooking and you taste the soup to see if it needs more salt. Paul is saying: day by day, situation by situation, learn what makes your Father smile. And do more of that.

---

## Relate It

You know how some people at school are just *themselves* — they don't try to be cool, they don't put on an act, they're just the same person in every room they walk into? And you can feel it. They're comfortable in their own skin.

The reason that stands out is because most people are fake. They're different around their friends than they are around their parents. Different in public than they are alone. Like a thousand different versions of one person.

Being a child of light means you don't have to be fake anymore. You don't have to be one person on Sunday morning and a different person on Friday night. Because the light is *in* you. It's not a costume you put on for church. It's who you really are now.

---

## Illustrate It

When I was a kid, we had this old lamp in our living room that barely worked. The bulb was so dim you could barely read by it. You'd walk in and think, "Is this light on or off?" It was the worst of both worlds — not bright enough to help you see, but it used electricity anyway.

A lot of Christians live like that lamp. They've got just enough Jesus to feel guilty about sin, but not enough to actually change anything. They're not totally dark, but they're not really light either. They're just... dim.

Paul says: No. You **are** light. Don't be a dim bulb. Walk like who you are. Let the light shine so people can actually see — see the goodness, the rightness, the truth of who God is.

---

## Apply It

Here's your challenge for today. It's practical, and you can do it right now:

**Ask yourself: What makes God smile?**

I know that sounds simple, but think about it. When you wake up tomorrow morning, what could you do that would genuinely please your Father in heaven?

- Maybe it's talking to Him before you pick up your phone.
- Maybe it's choosing kindness when sarcasm would be easier.
- Maybe it's being honest when lying would save your skin.
- Maybe it's walking away from something you know is wrong.

Paul says the goal of the Christian life is to "try to discern what is pleasing to the Lord." That's not complicated theology. It's just learning what your Dad likes and doing it because you love Him.

## Prayer for Today
