# Tuesday — The Swap

**Scripture Focus:** Ephesians 5:4

> *"Let there be no filthiness nor foolish talk nor crude joking, which are out of place, but instead let there be thanksgiving."*

---

Alright, day two. Yesterday we talked about all the garbage Paul says doesn't belong in our lives. But here's the thing about Paul — he never just says "stop it" and walks away. He always gives you the replacement.

Look at the end of verse 4. He says: no filthiness, no foolish talk, no crude joking — **"but instead let there be thanksgiving."**

The word "instead" is doing a lot of work there. Paul is saying you can't just empty the bad stuff out and leave a vacuum. If you just stop the crude jokes but don't replace them, the silence fills up with something worse — resentment, bitterness, that awkward tension where everybody's walking on eggshells.

**You have to swap it.**

A few years ago I decided to eat better. So I stopped buying chips and cookies. But you know what happened? I didn't just stop eating junk — I started eating more of other things. Some of those things were good (apples). Some were just *different* junk (pretzels, because somehow I convinced myself pretzels were health food). It didn't work until I actually replaced the junk with something better.

Paul says the replacement for trash talk is **thanksgiving**. The replacement for greed is **gratitude**. The replacement for lust is **worship** — because worship is just giving thanks to the One who gave you everything, instead of craving what He hasn't given you.

---

## Relate It

Think about the last time you were with your friends and the conversation started going somewhere you knew it shouldn't. You know that feeling — that little check in your spirit? What if instead of going along with it, you turned it around?

I'm not saying you have to be the sermon guy. Nobody likes the guy who makes everybody feel awkward. But what if you just said, "Man, that's wild. Anyway — hey, thanks for helping me with that thing yesterday"? Just pivot to gratitude.

Gratitude is the reset button for your heart. You can't be grimy and grateful at the same time. They don't fit together.

---

## Illustrate It

Imagine you're holding a glass of dirty water. Someone offers you a glass of clean, cold, fresh water. You can't take it unless you put the dirty one down first, right? You can't hold both.

But here's the thing — most people try. They want the clean water, but they keep clutching the dirty glass with one hand while reaching for the clean one with the other. And they end up spilling both.

Paul is saying: set down the dirty glass. Open your hands. Now take the good one.

A thankful heart doesn't have room for filthy talk. A grateful person doesn't spend all day wanting what they don't have. The two can't coexist.

---

## Apply It

Here's your challenge for today:

**For the next 24 hours, every time you catch yourself complaining, gossiping, or joining in on trashy talk — stop and say one thing you're thankful for out loud.**

Even if you have to say it under your breath. Even if it feels weird. Train your mouth to default to thanks instead of trash.

It's like learning an instrument. Awkward at first. But eventually it becomes natural.

Try it at lunch today. Somebody starts talking about someone else — and you just say, "You know what, I'm really glad we get to eat today. I was hungry." Watch what happens. It's like turning on a light in a dim room.

---

## Prayer for Today

*God, I don't want a heart that's always wanting more or a mouth that tears things down. Teach me to be thankful — really thankful — for what I already have. Help me swap the garbage for gratitude today. Amen.*
