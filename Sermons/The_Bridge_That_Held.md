# The Bridge That Held
### Learning to Be the Church When Being the Church Costs Something
**Hebrews 10:23–25 (ESV)**

*Abundant Life Family Worship Center — Pastor Jason*

---

> **Big Idea:** God tested the foundation of this church, and you held — not because you were perfect, but because you were His, and you refused to let go of each other.

---

## Introduction

Do you know how they test a bridge before they let anyone drive on it? They bring out the heaviest trucks they can find and position them at the most vulnerable points. They load the bridge with more weight than it will ever carry in normal use. The test looks like it's trying to break the bridge. But the test is what *proves* the bridge. When it stands — that's when the builder knows: this bridge can carry what it was designed to carry.

For the past several months, God brought a load onto this church that none of us were sure we could carry. *What if the test wasn't meant to break us — but to prove us?*

I'll be honest with you this morning. When I got that phone call back in January about two people living out of their car — days before a winter storm — my first reaction wasn't faith. It was dread. I sat with them. I saw how they looked, how they talked, how they acted. And I knew bringing them into this church was going to cause problems.

And here's the part I haven't told many of you. I had already decided what I was going to recommend to the leadership. I was going to say no. Take the easy way out. But the more I prayed, the more convicted I felt. And what I realized was this: it wasn't a question about them. It was a question about *me*. Was I going to be obedient to what God was asking, or was I going to take the easy way out?

I was right about the problems. They came. But I was wrong about what the problems meant. They weren't a sign we were doing something wrong. They were a sign the load had arrived. And the question was never really about those two individuals — it was about *us*. Would the bridge hold?

The writer of Hebrews knew about a congregation under pressure — tempted to let go, to drift, to pull apart. Listen to what the Spirit is saying to us this morning.

> *"Let us hold fast the confession of our hope without wavering, for he who promised is faithful. And let us consider how to stir up one another to love and good works, not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another, and all the more as you see the Day drawing near."*
> **— Hebrews 10:23–25**

Notice every command is in the plural. *"Let US hold fast."* *"Let US consider one another."* The bridge holds because the church holds *together*.

This morning I want us to see three things:

**I. The Grip That Held — We held fast because He is faithful.**

**II. The Family That Stayed — We considered one another even when it was hard.**

**III. The Future That Awaits — God is not done with this church.**

---

## I. The Grip That Held

> *"Let us hold fast the confession of our hope without wavering, for he who promised is faithful."*

Let me say something that needs to be said clearly. What we went through was hard. There were mornings I didn't know how I was going to hold things together. There were conversations where I could hear the strain in your voice. There were confrontations in the parking lot. Dogs that got loose and children who got hurt. And some of you told me you weren't sure you could keep doing this.

I want to say this to all of you: I heard every one of those concerns. They were valid. You had real reasons to be frustrated, to be angry, to question whether this was right.

The word *"hold fast"* — it's the kind of grip you use when the rope is the only thing between you and the drop. And what kept that grip? *"For He who promised is faithful."* Not *"for you were strong enough."* You held fast because Someone else was holding you.

Now, we weren't perfect. We made mistakes. There were moments of frustration that should have been moments of grace. But here's what matters: you never let go. You didn't leave. You didn't walk out. You stayed. And staying — when staying is hard — that's what holding fast looks like.

I think about some of our people — those who were confronted directly, face to face, with cussing and threats — and they didn't escalate. They called the leadership and they held fast. That was the body of Christ working the way it's supposed to. I want to thank you for that.

*What does holding fast look like for you this week?* Not in a crisis — the ordinary faithfulness that comes after the test. The bridge that held under the load is the same bridge that carries everyday traffic. The grip isn't yours to manufacture, it's yours to maintain. And the One who kept you through this will keep you through whatever comes next. *He who promised is faithful.*

---

## II. The Family That Stayed

> *"And let us consider how to stir up one another to love and good works."*

You cannot hold fast in isolation. The people around you are part of how you hold on. The word *"consider"* means to fix your attention on something. And the writer says: fix your attention on one another. Not just to notice, but to *stir up* — to spur on — to love and good works.

Now, here's what usually happens when a church brings in someone who's hard to love. Some people get angry and pull away. Others try to help but do it alone, and the body fractures. Both break what God is trying to build.

But that's not what happened here.

Two of our little girls were at a church event. One of their dogs got loose and bit them. That moment could have split this church in half. A lot of people were angry. Rightfully so.

But I will never forget what happened next. The father and mother of those little girls came and said: *"We don't blame them. We know what the church is trying to do. We're okay."*

I don't think you understand what you did when you said that. You could have demanded they be removed. You could have insisted the church was wrong. It would have been understandable. But you *considered* the body. You thought about what your response would mean for the rest of us. And you chose grace.

> *"If one member suffers, all suffer together; if one member is honored, all rejoice together."* — **1 Corinthians 12:26**

We suffered together. But we stayed one body.

*Who in this church family needs you to "consider" them this week?* Not the easy person to love — the one who challenges you. Because if we only love the people who are easy to love, we'll never reach the people who are hard to reach. Thank you to every one of you who chose to stay connected rather than pull away. You were being the body when the body was under stress.

---

## III. The Future That Awaits

Now, if I stop at points one and two, I've told you to hold fast and consider one another — and that's good. But it could feel like more weight. So I don't stop there. The writer doesn't either.

> *"...not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some, but encouraging one another, and all the more as you see the Day drawing near."*

Do you see that phrase? *"All the more as you see the Day drawing near."* The writer is looking forward. Christ is returning. Everything we've been through — every difficult act of love, every choice to stay — it's all building toward something.

And here's what I want to tell you this morning. Since this season passed — since the bridge held — I'm seeing something I haven't seen in a while. People on fire for the Lord that I haven't seen in some time. Phone calls from people I've never talked to before, asking about our church. People God is bringing back.

> *"I know your works. Behold, I have set before you an open door, which no one is able to shut."* — **Revelation 3:8**

I believe God is opening a door for this church. Not because we were perfect — we weren't. But because we held fast. Because we stayed together.

Remember the bridge. The load test isn't the end — it's the beginning. After the test, the bridge opens to traffic. It does what it was designed to do. The test wasn't the purpose. The purpose is what comes after.

*Watch. Pray. Be ready.* God is going to use this church in ways we haven't seen yet, and He needs the same thing from us He needed during this season: a people who hold fast, who consider one another, who encourage each other — all the more as we see the Day approaching.

---

## Conclusion

That bridge that held — when the test is done, they don't leave the trucks on it. They open it. And it carries people where they need to go.

The question was: *What if the test wasn't meant to break us — but to prove us?* We've been proven. The bridge held.

And now God wants to carry people across this church — lost people, broken people, people like the ones we took in. He wants to use *you* to reach them. To baptize them. To disciple them. To send them out.

Because that's what happened. One of them made a decision for Christ. The other soon followed. Both of them baptized. Two souls dead in trespasses — alive now — because this church was willing to be the church.

I want to close with a personal word of thanks.

Thank you to the one who had the compassion to make that phone call.

Thank you to those who were confronted directly — who handled a hard situation with grace and trusted the leadership.

Thank you to the parents of those little girls — you chose to release the offense and keep your hearts soft. That was the body of Christ at its finest.

Thank you to everyone who kept coming, who kept serving, who kept praying, who didn't walk away.

And most of all — thank You, Father, for being faithful.

*Are you ready for what God wants to do next?*

## Response / Invitation

1. **If you are** — then don't settle back into comfort. You've been tested and proven. Now go — hold fast, consider one another, encourage — and be the church God has shaped you to be.

2. **If you are not** — if your grip is weak this morning, if you've never surrendered your life to Jesus Christ — hear this: the same faithful God who held this church together is reaching out to you today. He who promised is faithful. Will you let Him hold you?
