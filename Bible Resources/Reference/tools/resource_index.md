# Bible Study Tools — Reference Guide

> Quick-reference cards for PDF-based study tools available under `Ministry/Bible Resources/`. These are full reference works in PDF format — this guide summarizes what each contains and how to use it in sermon/study prep.

---

## Vine's Expository Dictionary

**File:** `Bible Resources/Vines-Expositary-Dictionary.pdf` (6.8 MB)

**What it is:** W.E. Vine's classic expository dictionary of Old and New Testament words. Provides Greek (NT) and Hebrew (OT) word studies with biblical usage, definitions, and theological significance.

**How to use:** When studying a passage, look up key words in their original language. Vine's shows the Greek/Hebrew word behind the English translation, its root meaning, and how it's used across different contexts.

**Example:** The Greek word *agapao* (love) vs. *phileo* (brotherly love) — Vine's explains the distinction with scriptural references.

---

## Nave's Topical Bible

**File:** `Bible Resources/Nave's Topical Bible.pdf` (18 MB)

**What it is:** Orville Nave's exhaustive topical concordance. Over 20,000 topics with 100,000+ Scripture references organized by subject.

**How to use:** When building a sermon or study on a theme (e.g., "Grace," "Faith," "The Church"), look up the topic in Nave's to find every relevant Bible passage organized systematically.

---

## Topical Analysis of the Bible

**File:** `Bible Resources/Topical Analysis of the Bible.pdf` (37.7 MB)

**What it is:** A systematic doctrinal and thematic analysis of the entire Bible, organized by topic and theological category.

**How to use:** For doctrinal studies or teaching series, use this to find how a doctrine develops across Scripture.

---

## A Greek-English Lexicon of the New Testament

**File:** `Bible Resources/A Greek-English Lexicon of the New Testament.pdf` (95.5 MB)

**What it is:** Comprehensive Greek-English lexicon (equivalent to BDAG-level reference). Every Greek word in the NT with definitions, usage examples, and grammatical forms.

**How to use:** When you need definitive word studies at the Greek level. More detailed than Vine's — gives full semantic range.

---

## Theological Wordbook of the Old Testament

**File:** `Bible Resources/Theological Wordbook of the Old Testament.pdf` (51.3 MB)

**What it is:** Comprehensive Hebrew word study resource covering the Old Testament vocabulary with theological commentary on each word.

**How to use:** For OT sermon/study prep, look up Hebrew key words for deeper understanding of their meaning and theological significance.
