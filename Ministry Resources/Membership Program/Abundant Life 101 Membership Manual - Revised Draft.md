# Welcome and How to Use This Manual

This manual is designed for guests, regular attenders, and prospective members of Abundant Life Family Worship Center. Its purpose is to explain what we believe, how we seek to make disciples, and what church membership means in the life of this local body.

Our aim is clarity, not pressure. We want you to understand the gospel we preach, the doctrine we teach, the mission we share, and the covenant commitment involved in membership. Joining a church should not be a casual decision. It should be a joyful and serious commitment to Christ and to His people.

The content is organized into four teaching sessions. These sessions may be taught on Sunday mornings, in a small group setting, or over a weekend.

## Recommended Class Format

- Read the assigned pages before class.
- Bring a Bible and take notes.
- Ask honest questions.
- Attend all sessions of Abundant Life 101.
- Complete a membership conversation with a pastor or elder.
- If you are ready to pursue membership, review and sign the Membership Covenant.

## Key Biblical Themes

Throughout this manual we will return to several biblical themes:

- The gospel: Christ's life, death, resurrection, and reign. See 1 Corinthians 15:1-4.
- The ordinary means of grace: the Word, prayer, and the ordinances. See Acts 2:42.
- A regenerate church: believers gathering in covenant, accountability, and love. See Hebrews 10:24-25.
- Mission: making disciples as ordinary Christians in everyday life. See Matthew 28:18-20.

# Membership Summary

Membership at Abundant Life Family Worship Center means we are saying something together. The church is saying, "These are the people we are responsible to shepherd." The member is saying, "This is my church family, and these are my leaders."

Membership is not a consumer relationship. It is not mainly about preference, programs, or convenience. It is a covenant commitment to follow Christ together as a local church family.

## Membership at Abundant Life Means:

- I have repented of sin and trusted Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior.
- I have been baptized as a believer by immersion, or I am willing to pursue believer's baptism.
- I affirm and will not knowingly oppose the church's doctrine, worship, mission, and biblical leadership.
- I commit to gather, grow, serve, give, reconcile, and live on mission with this church family.
- I welcome biblical shepherding, accountability, and care.
- I want Abundant Life Family Worship Center to be my church family.

## Key Membership Commitments

As members, we commit ourselves, by God's grace, to:

- Worship faithfully with the church.
- Pursue holiness and repentance.
- Refuse gossip and pursue reconciliation.
- Serve the body with our gifts.
- Support the mission of the church.
- Submit to biblical shepherding.
- Participate in the life and decisions of the church as responsible members.

We do not make these commitments because we are perfect. We make them because Christ has saved us, placed us in His body, and called us to walk together in truth and love.

# Session 1: The Gospel, Our Mission, and Our Doctrine

This session answers three questions:

1. What is the gospel?
2. What is our mission and vision?
3. What do we believe and teach as a church?

## 1. The Gospel We Proclaim

We proclaim the good news that God saves sinners by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone.

God is holy, righteous, and good. He created all things for His glory, and every person is accountable to Him. Yet mankind has sinned against God. We have not loved Him as we should. We have not obeyed Him as we should. We are guilty before Him and unable to save ourselves.

But God, being rich in mercy, sent His Son. Jesus Christ, fully God and fully man, lived in perfect obedience, died a substitutionary death for sinners, rose bodily from the grave, and reigns as Lord. The gospel calls every person to repent of sin and trust in Christ.

Salvation is not earned by our works. It is received by grace through faith. Those whom God saves, He forgives, makes new, brings into His family, and begins to conform to the image of Christ.

Key texts:

- 1 Corinthians 15:1-4
- Romans 3:21-26
- Ephesians 2:8-10
- Acts 17:30-31

## 2. Our Mission

Abundant Life Family Worship Center exists to glorify God by proclaiming the gospel of Jesus Christ, gathering and shepherding a regenerate church, and making disciples across Carroll County through the ordinary means of grace, so that the unchurched are converted, the hurting are restored, and families are built up in truth and love.

## 3. Our Vision

We envision a Christ-exalting, Scripture-shaped church family in Carroll County where unchurched neighbors are welcomed, convicted and comforted by the Word, made alive by the Spirit, and formed into joyful disciples.

We want to be marked by reverent worship, gospel unity, recovery and restoration, and a rising generation grounded in Christ and sent into daily life on mission.

## 4. Doctrinal Summary

The following summary does not replace Scripture. Scripture alone is our final authority. This summary simply explains the major truths we teach and confess together.

### Scripture

We believe the Bible is inspired by God, true in all that it teaches, and sufficient for faith and godliness. Scripture is the final authority over the church, its doctrine, its worship, and its life.

### The Triune God

We believe there is one true and living God who eternally exists in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. These three are one God, equal in glory, power, and deity.

### Creation, Humanity, and Sin

We believe God created mankind in His image. Man was created good, but through Adam's sin, mankind fell. Every person is now sinful by nature and by choice, guilty before God, and in need of salvation.

### Jesus Christ

We believe Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God. He is fully God and fully man. He was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary, lived without sin, died as a substitute for sinners, rose bodily from the dead, ascended to the Father, and will return in glory.

### Salvation

We believe salvation is by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone. God saves sinners through the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ. The proper response to the gospel is repentance toward God and faith in the Lord Jesus Christ.

### The Holy Spirit

We believe the Holy Spirit regenerates sinners, indwells believers, unites them to Christ, sanctifies them, gives gifts for service, and empowers the church for holiness and witness.

### The Church

We believe the local church is a covenant community of baptized believers who gather under Christ's Word, practice the ordinances, care for one another, exercise discipline, and carry out the Great Commission.

### Baptism and the Lord's Supper

We believe Christ gave two ordinances to His church: baptism and the Lord's Supper. Baptism is for believers and is practiced by immersion as a public declaration of faith in Christ. The Lord's Supper is a church ordinance in which believers remember Christ's death, proclaim His gospel, and examine themselves in faith and repentance.

### Christian Holiness and Perseverance

We believe those whom God saves are called to pursue holiness. The Christian life is marked by repentance, faith, obedience, love, and perseverance. We do not pursue holiness to earn salvation, but because Christ has saved us and His Spirit is at work in us.

### The Return of Christ, Judgment, and New Creation

We believe Jesus Christ will return visibly and bodily. The dead will be raised. There will be final judgment. The wicked will face eternal judgment, and the redeemed will dwell with God forever in the new heavens and new earth.

## 5. Church Distinctives

Several convictions shape our life together:

- Expository preaching and Scripture-saturated worship.
- Regenerate church membership.
- Meaningful membership and loving church discipline.
- Believer's baptism by immersion.
- The Lord's Supper as an ordinance of the gathered church.
- Elder-led shepherding with congregational accountability.
- Discipleship through the ordinary means of grace: the Word, prayer, ordinances, fellowship, and mutual care.

## 6. How We Use Our Articles of Faith

The Articles of Faith in Appendix B were adopted in an earlier season of the church's life. We honor the biblical truths they affirm, while also clarifying how we currently teach and shepherd on several matters.

Scripture alone is our final authority. Doctrinal statements and confessional summaries are useful only insofar as they faithfully summarize Scripture. We want to be clear where Scripture is clear, careful where faithful Christians have differed, and united around the gospel and the mission Christ has given His church.

## 7. Doctrinal Clarifications

Because our adopted Articles of Faith come from an earlier season of the church's life, several areas need present pastoral clarification.

### Baptism in the Holy Spirit

We affirm that every believer is baptized in the Holy Spirit at conversion. By the Spirit, believers are united to Christ and made members of His body. We also affirm that believers should continually seek the Spirit's filling and empowerment for holiness, obedience, and witness.

Key texts:

- 1 Corinthians 12:13
- Ephesians 5:18

### Divine Healing

We believe God hears the prayers of His people, and we pray expectantly for healing. We also trust God's wisdom and providence when He chooses not to heal in this life.

We do not teach that physical healing is promised to every believer in the atonement in the same way forgiveness of sin is promised to all who repent and believe. Christ will finally heal every sickness and remove every sorrow in the resurrection and the new creation.

Key texts:

- James 5:14-16
- 2 Corinthians 12:7-10
- Revelation 21:1-5

### End Times

We affirm the visible return of Christ, the resurrection of the dead, final judgment, and the new heavens and new earth.

On millennial and rapture details, members may hold differing orthodox views without division. We ask members to remain humble, teachable, submissive to Scripture, and careful not to make secondary end-times details a source of conflict in the body.

### The Doctrines of Grace

We affirm God's sovereign grace in salvation. God saves sinners by His mercy, not by human merit. We gladly confess that salvation belongs to the Lord, and we also gladly call every person to repent and believe the gospel.

These truths should make us humble, prayerful, evangelistic, and grateful.

## Discussion Questions

2. What part of the church's mission and vision stands out to you most?
3. What questions do you have about our doctrine or distinctives?

# Session 2: Our Discipleship Pathway and Ministry Strategy

This session explains how we pursue our mission in practical church life. Our strategy is not program-first. We want our ministries to serve the discipleship of real people, not replace the ordinary work Christ has given His church.

## 1. Discipleship Pathway

We summarize our discipleship pathway in four movements:

### Know Christ

We pray and labor for conversion through gospel proclamation, personal witness, hospitality, and prayer. We want sinners to hear the gospel clearly, repent of sin, and trust in Jesus Christ.

### Join the Church

Believers are baptized and received into membership so they can be shepherded, strengthened, and accountable. We do not want people to drift around the edges of church life. We want believers to belong to a local body where they are known and cared for.

### Grow in Grace

We grow through the Word, prayer, the Lord's Supper, fellowship, service, Scripture intake, and mutual encouragement. Growth is not instant. It is the steady work of God through the means He has promised to bless.

### Serve and Send

Every member is equipped to serve the body and live on mission in daily life. Ministry is not only what happens on a platform. Ministry happens when members love one another, teach children, greet guests, visit the hurting, share the gospel, pray, give, and serve faithfully.

Key texts:

- Matthew 28:18-20
- Acts 2:42-47
- Colossians 1:28-29
- 1 Peter 4:10-11

## 2. The Ordinary Means of Grace

Our methodology is intentionally simple. We rely on what Christ has promised to bless in the local church.

### The Word

God saves, sanctifies, corrects, comforts, and equips His people through His Word. That is why preaching, teaching, reading, and applying Scripture are central to our life together.

### Prayer

Prayer is not a decoration on ministry. Prayer is dependence on God. We pray because only God can save sinners, restore the broken, heal the wounded, and strengthen His church.

### The Ordinances

Baptism and the Lord's Supper visibly proclaim the gospel. Baptism marks out believers as followers of Christ. The Lord's Supper strengthens the church as we remember Christ's death and await His return.

### Fellowship and Mutual Care

The Christian life is not meant to be lived alone. We need brothers and sisters who will encourage us, correct us, pray for us, bear burdens with us, and remind us of the gospel.

## 3. Reaching the Unchurched in Carroll County

We assume many of our neighbors are not actively connected to a local church, even in a region with many church buildings. Our aim is to be a clear, compassionate, and courageous gospel witness.

We want to build trust through service and relationships, but we never want to hide the call to repentance and faith.

Members help the church reach the unchurched by:

- Practicing hospitality.
- Inviting neighbors, coworkers, friends, and family.
- Sharing the gospel in ordinary conversations.
- Following up with guests.
- Serving in mercy and recovery ministries.
- Living with visible love and holiness.

## 4. Recovery and Restoration

Because the gospel addresses both guilt and bondage, we intentionally pursue restoration for those enslaved to sin and broken by suffering. Lasting change flows from union with Christ, repentance and faith, and ongoing discipleship in the local church.

Recovery ministry should never be detached from the church. It should be one expression of the church's shepherding, discipleship, and mission.

Our recovery and restoration work may include:

- A weekly recovery gathering that is safe, structured, and gospel-centered.
- A care team or trained mentors who provide accountability and practical support.
- Connection to the Lord's Day gathering.
- Pastoral care and wise accountability.
- A pathway toward baptism and membership when credible faith and stability are present.
- Service and reintegration into the body as restored believers strengthen others.

Key texts:

- Isaiah 61:1-3
- Galatians 6:1-2
- James 5:16
- 2 Corinthians 5:17

## 5. Youth and Family Discipleship

We aim to raise a generation grounded in Scripture, connected to the church family, and equipped to live as followers of Christ.

We believe parents are the primary disciplers of their children, and the church is a reinforcing family. Our youth and children's ministry should support the home, not replace it.

Our commitments include:

- Parents as primary disciplers. See Deuteronomy 6:4-9.
- A safe and joyful youth environment that supports holiness, service, and mission.
- Mentoring relationships with mature members of the church.
- Clear teaching on the gospel, doctrine, and Christian living.
- Careful child-safety practices and accountable leadership.

Key texts:

- Psalm 78:4
- 2 Timothy 2:2
- 1 Timothy 4:12

## Discussion Questions

1. Where do you currently see yourself in the discipleship pathway?
2. Who has helped you grow spiritually?
3. How can members help the church reach the unchurched without becoming program-driven?

# Session 3: Church Membership and Congregational Life

This session explains what membership means and how we live together as a church family.

## 1. What Is the Church?

The local church is a covenant community of baptized believers who gather under Christ's Word, live as a family, practice the ordinances, care for one another, and carry out His mission.

Before we talk about organization, we begin with identity. Scripture highlights three marks that shape our culture and decisions.

### Oneness: Unity

We are one body in Christ, committed to visible love, reconciliation, and shared life.

Key texts:

- Ephesians 4:1-6
- John 13:34-35

### Holiness: Purity

We are a holy people, set apart for God, pursuing repentance and obedience by the Spirit.

Key texts:

- 1 Peter 1:15-16
- Hebrews 12:14

### Truth: Verity

We are a truth-bearing people, receiving, guarding, and proclaiming the gospel and the whole counsel of God.

Key texts:

- 1 Timothy 3:15
- Jude 3

## 2. Why Membership Matters

Membership is the church's way of saying, "These are the people we are responsible to shepherd," and the member's way of saying, "This is my church family, and these are my leaders."

Membership gives clarity to love. It helps the church know who is under its care. It helps members know where they belong, who is shepherding them, and who they are called to love, serve, and walk with.

Membership is not perfection. It is not status. It is not a way to control people. Membership is a biblical, covenant commitment to follow Christ together.

## 3. Who May Become a Member?

A person may become a member of Abundant Life Family Worship Center if the following are true:

- They have a credible profession of faith in Jesus Christ and a desire to follow Him as Lord.
- They have been baptized as a believer by immersion, or they are willing to pursue believer's baptism.
- They are willing to support the church's doctrine, worship, and mission.
- They are willing to live under biblical shepherding and accountability.
- They are willing to sign the Membership Covenant.

Key texts:

- Romans 10:9-10
- Acts 2:41
- Romans 6:3-4
- Hebrews 13:17

## 4. Who May Need More Time Before Joining?

Not everyone who attends Abundant Life should join immediately. Sometimes the most loving thing is to slow down, ask questions, and seek clarity.

A person may need more time before joining if:

- They are still unsure about the gospel or their own profession of faith.
- They have not been baptized as a believer and are not yet willing to pursue baptism.
- They disagree with the church's doctrine in a way that would create division.
- They are unwilling to live under biblical shepherding and accountability.
- They are unresolved in serious conflict or discipline at another church.
- They are mainly seeking programs, influence, or personal preference rather than covenant commitment.

If this describes you, we are not trying to push you away. We want to walk with you honestly and patiently. The goal is not to rush people into membership. The goal is meaningful membership that honors Christ and cares for souls.

## 5. How Members Are Received

Our normal membership process is:

1. Attend Abundant Life 101.
2. Complete a membership conversation with a pastor or elder.
3. Share your testimony and baptism history.
4. Ask questions about doctrine, church life, or expectations.
5. If needed, schedule believer's baptism.
6. Read and sign the Membership Covenant.
7. Be recommended by the elder or elders.
8. Be received by the congregation at a members meeting.

## 6. What to Expect in the Membership Conversation

The membership conversation is not meant to be intimidating. It is a pastoral conversation for clarity, care, and shepherding.

The conversation will normally include:

- Your testimony of conversion.
- Your baptism.
- Your church background.
- Your understanding of the gospel.
- Any major doctrinal questions.
- Any areas where you desire shepherding, care, or discipleship.
- Possible areas where you may serve.

## 7. Duties and Privileges of Members

Membership carries real privileges and real responsibilities. Members receive care, shepherding, participation, and voice. Members also commit to worship, holiness, love, service, and mission.

### Members Commit To:

- Gather with the church on the Lord's Day as their normal pattern. See Hebrews 10:24-25.
- Participate reverently in the ordinances: baptism once and the Lord's Supper regularly.
- Pray for the church, its leaders, and its witness in Carroll County. See Colossians 4:2-4.
- Pursue holiness, repentance, and obedience as a pattern of life. See 1 Peter 1:15-16.
- Practice love, hospitality, and practical help. See Galatians 6:1-2.
- Refuse gossip and pursue reconciliation quickly and biblically. See Matthew 5:23-24 and Ephesians 4:29-32.
- Receive and support faithful preaching and teaching, testing all things by Scripture. See Acts 17:11.
- Use their gifts to serve the body under accountable leadership. See 1 Peter 4:10-11.
- Give regularly, proportionally, and cheerfully as the Lord enables. See 2 Corinthians 9:7.
- Live on mission as a witness to Christ in daily life. See Matthew 28:18-20.

Giving is not a way to earn standing before God or status in the church. Giving is an act of worship, stewardship, and participation in the mission of the church.

### Members Can Expect the Church To:

- Preach and teach Scripture faithfully.
- Pray for them and shepherd them.
- Administer baptism and the Lord's Supper with care.
- Help them pursue holiness and restoration.
- Provide accountable leadership.
- Treat sensitive matters with wisdom, privacy, and love.
- Equip them to serve and live on mission.

## 8. Membership Categories

These categories help us shepherd with clarity and care.

### Active Member

An active member is a member who normally gathers with the church on the Lord's Day, participates in the ordinances, remains in pastoral communication, contributes to the life and mission of the church as able, and seeks to live consistently with the Membership Covenant.

### Inactive Member

An inactive member is a member who has been absent from the gathered life of the church for an extended period, normally three to six months, without meaningful pastoral communication or providential reason.

### Member Under Pastoral Care

A member under pastoral care is a member receiving focused shepherding due to hardship, conflict, spiritual danger, or restoration needs. Such care should be handled with privacy, dignity, and biblical love.

### Removed or Transferred Member

A member may be removed or transferred through transfer to another faithful church, resignation, relocation, or, if necessary, church discipline.

## 9. Membership Care Pathway

When a member becomes absent, spiritually endangered, or difficult to contact, the church normally responds with patient and loving care.

Our normal care pathway is:

1. Informal check-in from a pastor, elder, deacon, or member care team.
2. Pastoral conversation to understand the situation.
3. Encouragement, prayer, and a clear next step.
4. Focused pastoral care or a restoration plan if needed.
5. Continued follow-up with patience and clarity.
6. If the member remains unresponsive or unrepentant, possible action by the church according to Scripture and the Membership Covenant.

## 10. Church Discipline and Restoration

Because Jesus loves His church, He gives real authority for the good of His people. Church discipline is not punishment. It is loving correction and careful restoration. It protects the church's witness, guards the flock, and calls wandering sheep back to Christ.

Most discipline in the church is informal and ordinary: members encouraging, correcting, forgiving, and restoring one another in love. Formal discipline is rare and reserved for serious, outward, and unrepentant sin.

### Purposes of Discipline

- The honor of Christ and the purity of His church. See 1 Corinthians 5:6-8.
- The restoration of the sinner through repentance and faith. See Galatians 6:1.
- The protection and peace of the congregation. See Acts 20:28-31.

### The Ordinary Steps

Matthew 18:15-20 gives the normal pattern:

1. Private conversation and correction when a brother or sister sins.
2. If unrepentant, one or two others join for confirmation and help.
3. If still unrepentant, the matter is brought to the church for prayer and action under elder leadership.
4. If there is final, settled unrepentance, the church may remove a person from membership, always holding out hope of restoration.

In cases involving abuse, criminal activity, or immediate danger, we involve civil authorities and take protective action without delay. We also aim to handle every case with careful documentation, pastoral humility, and appropriate confidentiality.

Key texts:

- Matthew 18:15-20
- 1 Corinthians 5
- 2 Thessalonians 3:14-15
- Galatians 6:1

# Session 4: Leadership, Worship, and Safeguards

This session explains how we organize our life together for faithful worship, healthy leadership, and wise care.

## 1. Christ the Head of the Church

Christ is the Head of the church. He purchased the church with His blood, rules the church by His Word, and shepherds the church through the leaders He gives.

No pastor, elder, deacon, member, team, or tradition owns the church. The church belongs to Jesus.

Key texts:

- Matthew 16:18
- Ephesians 1:22-23
- Colossians 1:18
- Acts 20:28

## 2. Elders, Pastors, and Overseers

Under Christ, Scripture recognizes the office of elder, also called pastor or overseer. These terms emphasize different aspects of the same office: shepherding, oversight, teaching, and care.

### Primary Responsibilities

Elders are responsible for:

- Prayer.
- Preaching and teaching.
- Shepherding the flock.
- Guarding doctrine.
- Equipping the saints.
- Overseeing the direction and health of the church.

Key texts:

- Acts 6:4
- Acts 20:28-31
- 1 Timothy 3:1-7
- 1 Timothy 5:17
- Titus 1:5-9
- 1 Peter 5:1-4

### Qualifications

Elders must be men of godly character, household faithfulness, spiritual maturity, and ability to teach.

### Current Structure and Goal

We presently have one elder. We will actively identify and train qualified men toward a plurality of elders as the Lord provides.

In a church our size, a healthy goal is a plurality of elders with a team of deacons who strengthen ministry and care.

## 3. Deacons

Scripture also recognizes the office of deacon. Deacons are servant leaders who strengthen the practical ministry of the church, protect unity, and free the elder or elders to give themselves to prayer and the ministry of the Word.

### Primary Responsibilities

Deacons may serve in areas such as:

- Mercy and recovery care.
- Facilities and property.
- Finance and stewardship.
- Hospitality and member care.
- Practical needs that protect unity and support the mission.

### Qualifications

Deacons must be tested, faithful, dignified, honest, and full of faith.

Key texts:

- Acts 6:1-7
- 1 Timothy 3:8-13

## 4. Ministry Teams

In addition to officers, we may appoint ministry coordinators and teams who serve under the oversight of the elder or elders and, when applicable, related deacons.

Possible ministry teams include:

### Worship Ministry Team

Supports congregational singing, Scripture reading, and service flow. This team prioritizes doctrinally sound content, reverent joy, and the voice of the congregation over performance.

### Oneness or Member Care Team

Supports hospitality, follow-up, prayer care, meals, and practical help. This team strengthens unity and helps ensure no one is overlooked.

### Recovery and Restoration Team

Provides gospel-centered support and accountability for those seeking freedom from addiction and destructive patterns. This work must remain connected to pastoral care and clear safeguards.

### Youth Discipleship Team

Supports parents, teaches the gospel and doctrine, mentors students, and maintains rigorous child-safety policies.

### Hospitality and Guest Follow-Up Team

Welcomes guests, helps them understand the church, and provides timely follow-up after visits.

## 5. Congregational Life and Decision-Making

We practice elder-led, congregationally accountable church life. Elders lead and shepherd. The congregation participates and affirms key matters.

Until formal bylaws are adopted, these operating guidelines define our normal practice.

### Matters Normally Brought to the Congregation

- Receiving and removing members, including discipline cases.
- Affirming elders and deacons, including call, installation, and removal when necessary.
- Approving an annual budget and major non-budgeted expenditures.
- Major direction changes, including property decisions, mergers, or significant ministry expansions.

## 6. Members Meetings

We aim to hold regular members meetings, at least quarterly if possible. These meetings should include prayer, ministry updates, member care, receiving or removing members, and stewardship decisions.

We seek unity and clarity. When votes are required, we aim for informed, Scripture-governed decisions rather than hurried reactions.

As the church grows, we intend to draft and adopt a constitution and bylaws that formalize these practices with greater detail and legal clarity.

## 7. Gathered Worship

Because worship is central to the church's life, we aim to worship God as He commands in Scripture, with reverence and joy. Our services are Word-centered and congregation-led, supported by musicians rather than performed for spectators.

### Core Principles

- God-centered: the aim is God's glory, not entertainment.
- Scripture-governed: we prioritize what Scripture commands and avoid novelty that distracts from the gospel.
- Congregational: the gathered church sings, prays, listens, gives, and responds together.
- Reverent and joyful: we approach God with awe, gratitude, and gladness.

### Elements We Ordinarily Include

- Reading of Scripture.
- Expository preaching.
- Prayer: adoration, confession, thanksgiving, and intercession.
- Congregational singing of biblically faithful songs and psalms.
- Baptism and the Lord's Supper.
- Giving as an act of worship and stewardship.

Key texts:

- Acts 2:42
- Colossians 3:16
- Ephesians 5:19
- 1 Corinthians 14:26, 40

## 8. Practical Policies and Safeguards

Love and order protect the weak and strengthen trust. The following practices are expected in all ministries.

### Child and Youth Safety

We seek to provide a safe environment for children and students through screened volunteers, clear supervision practices, accountable leadership, and mandatory reporting when required by law.

### Financial Integrity

We seek transparent reporting, dual-control counting, documented approvals for expenditures, and wise stewardship of the resources entrusted to the church.

### Teaching Accountability

Teachers, small-group leaders, and ministry leaders should support the church's doctrine and submit to pastoral oversight.

### Confidentiality and Care

We protect sensitive information while seeking appropriate help and wise counsel when needed. Confidentiality is important, but it does not prevent appropriate reporting, safety action, or pastoral accountability when required.

### Separate Ministry Policies

Some ministry policies, such as a temporary shelter policy, child-safety policy, or financial policy, should be kept as separate documents. They should be reviewed carefully before adoption and should not be treated as part of this membership manual unless formally approved by the church.

## Discussion Questions

1. Why does leadership structure matter for the health of the church?
2. How should members think about congregational responsibility?
3. What does Scripture-shaped worship look like in practice?

# Membership Covenant

Having received Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior, and having been baptized upon a credible profession of faith, we now commit ourselves to one another, by God's grace, to walk together in the life of this church.

We understand that this covenant is not a claim of perfection. It is a sincere commitment to walk together in truth, love, repentance, service, and mission.

## 1. We Will Protect the Unity of the Church

- By praying for one another and refusing gossip.
- By pursuing reconciliation quickly and biblically when conflicts arise.
- By speaking the truth in love.
- By supporting biblical shepherding with a teachable spirit.

## 2. We Will Share the Mission of the Church

- By living as witnesses to the gospel in our homes, workplaces, schools, and community.
- By welcoming the unchurched with hospitality and clarity.
- By supporting recovery and restoration ministries with truth and compassion.

# Appendix B: Articles of Faith and Doctrinal Clarifications

## Historical Articles of Faith

The following Articles of Faith were adopted in an earlier season of the church's life. They are included here for transparency and historical continuity.

1. The Bible is inspired by God and is "the infallible, authoritative rule of faith and conduct."

2. There is only one true God who exists as a Trinity.

3. Jesus Christ is the Son of God and, as the second person of the Trinity, is God.

4. Man was created good by God but was separated from God through original sin.

5. Salvation "is received through repentance toward God and faith toward the Lord Jesus Christ."

6. There are two ordinances. Believer's baptism by immersion is a declaration to the world of the believer's faith in Christ. The Lord's Supper is a symbolic remembrance of Christ's suffering and death.

7. Baptism in the Holy Spirit is a separate and subsequent experience following conversion. Spirit baptism brings empowerment to live an overcoming Christian life and to be an effective witness.

8. Sanctification is "an act of separation from that which is evil, and of dedication unto God." It occurs when the believer identifies with, and has faith in, Christ in his death and resurrection. It is understood to be a process in that it requires continual yielding to the Holy Spirit.

9. The Church's mission is to seek and save all who are lost in sin; the Church is the Body of Christ and consists of all people who accept Christ, regardless of Christian denomination.

10. Divinely called and scripturally-ordained ministers serve the Church.

11. Divine healing of the sick is provided for in the atonement.

12. The "imminent and blessed hope" of the Church is its rapture preceding the bodily return of Christ to earth.

13. The rapture of the Church will be followed by the visible return of Christ and his reign on earth for a thousand years.

14. There will be a final judgment and eternal damnation for the "wicked dead."

15. There will be future new heavens and a new earth "wherein dwelleth righteousness."

## Clarifying Note

We honor the biblical truths affirmed in these Articles, especially the authority of Scripture, the Trinity, the deity of Christ, the reality of sin, salvation through repentance and faith, the ordinances, the mission of the church, final judgment, and the new creation.

At the same time, several points require present clarification. These clarifications are not meant to create confusion. They are meant to help the church teach with biblical care and unity.

## Current Doctrinal Clarifications

### Scripture as Final Authority

Scripture alone is the final authority for the church. Doctrinal statements are useful only when they faithfully summarize what Scripture teaches.

### Spirit Baptism

We currently teach that every believer is baptized in the Holy Spirit at conversion. We also teach that believers should continually seek the Spirit's filling and empowerment for holiness and witness.

### Divine Healing

We pray for healing and trust God's power. We also trust His wisdom when healing does not come in this life. We do not teach that physical healing is guaranteed to every believer in the same way forgiveness is guaranteed to all who repent and trust Christ.

### End-Times Details

We affirm Christ's visible return, resurrection, final judgment, and new creation. We do not require members to agree on every millennial or rapture detail, provided they remain faithful to Scripture and do not create division.

### Church Polity

We pursue elder-led and congregationally accountable church life. Elders lead and shepherd, and the congregation affirms key matters such as membership, officers, discipline, and major stewardship decisions.

### Ordinances

We practice believer's baptism by immersion for those who credibly profess faith. We celebrate the Lord's Supper as a church ordinance for baptized believers walking in repentance and faith.

### Doctrines of Grace

We affirm God's sovereign grace in salvation. These truths should lead us to worship, humility, evangelism, prayer, and confidence in God's power to save.

## Long-Term Recommendation

The church should consider adopting a revised doctrinal statement, constitution, and bylaws that clearly reflect the church's current teaching, polity, and membership practice.
