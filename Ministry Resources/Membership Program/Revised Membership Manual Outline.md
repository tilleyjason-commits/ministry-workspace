# Revised Outline for Abundant Life 101 Membership Manual

Abundant Life Family Worship Center
Cedar Grove, Tennessee

## Purpose of the Revised Manual

This revised manual should help guests, regular attenders, and prospective members understand three things clearly:

1. What the gospel is and what Abundant Life believes.
2. What membership means as a covenant commitment to Christ and His church.
3. What the practical process is for becoming, remaining, and growing as a member.

The manual should be warm, clear, biblical, and usable in a membership class setting. It should avoid sounding like a policy handbook while still giving enough structure to guide the church well.

# Welcome and How to Use This Manual

## Purpose

Explain that the manual is for guests, new attenders, and prospective members who want to understand Abundant Life's doctrine, mission, values, and membership expectations.

## Recommended Class Format

- Read assigned sections before class.
- Bring a Bible and questions.
- Attend each session of Abundant Life 101.
- Complete a membership conversation with an elder/pastor.
- Review and sign the Membership Covenant if ready to pursue membership.

## Tone to Emphasize

- Clarity, not pressure.
- Biblical conviction with pastoral warmth.

# Membership Summary

Add a one-page summary near the beginning.

## Membership at Abundant Life Means:

- I have repented of sin and trusted Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior.
- I have been baptized as a believer by immersion, or I am willing to pursue believer's baptism.

- I commit to gather, grow, serve, give, reconcile, and live on mission with this church family.

- Worship faithfully.
- Pursue holiness.
- Refuse gossip and pursue reconciliation.
- Serve the body.
- Support the mission.
- Submit to biblical shepherding.
- Participate in the life and decisions of the church as a responsible member.

# Session 1: The Gospel, Our Mission, and Our Doctrine

## 1. The Gospel We Proclaim

Keep the current gospel section, but make it the theological foundation for the entire manual.

Include:

- God is holy and Creator.
- Man is sinful and unable to save himself.
- Jesus Christ is fully God and fully man.
- Christ lived perfectly, died substitutionally, rose bodily, and reigns as Lord.
- Salvation is by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone.
- The proper response is repentance and faith.

Key texts:

- 1 Corinthians 15:1-4
- Romans 3:21-26
- Ephesians 2:8-10
- Acts 17:30-31

## 2. Our Mission

Use the current mission statement, possibly shortened for readability.

Suggested wording:

Abundant Life Family Worship Center exists to glorify God by proclaiming the gospel of Jesus Christ, gathering and shepherding a regenerate church, and making disciples across Carroll County through the ordinary means of grace, so that the unchurched are converted, the hurting are restored, and families are built up in truth and love.

## 3. Our Vision

Use the current vision statement, but connect it to the historical vision in Appendix A.

## 4. Doctrinal Summary

Summarize the church's doctrine under major headings:

- Scripture
- The Triune God
- Creation, humanity, and sin
- The person and work of Christ
- Salvation by grace through faith
- The Holy Spirit
- The church
- Baptism and the Lord's Supper
- Christian holiness and perseverance
- The return of Christ, judgment, and new creation

## 5. Church Distinctives

Keep this concise and practical:

- Expository preaching and Scripture-saturated worship.
- Regenerate church membership.
- Meaningful membership and loving church discipline.
- Believer's baptism by immersion.
- The Lord's Supper as a church ordinance.
- Elder-led shepherding with congregational accountability.
- Discipleship through the ordinary means of grace.

## 6. How We Use Our Articles of Faith

Add a clearer bridge between the older adopted Articles of Faith and the church's current teaching.

Suggested wording:

The Articles of Faith in Appendix B were adopted in an earlier season of the church's life. We honor the biblical truths they affirm, while also clarifying how we currently teach and shepherd on several matters. Scripture alone is our final authority. Historic confessional summaries and doctrinal statements are useful only insofar as they faithfully summarize Scripture.

## 7. Doctrinal Clarifications

Add short clarifications on areas where the older Articles need explanation.

### Baptism in the Holy Spirit

Clarify that every believer is baptized in the Spirit at conversion and should continually seek the Spirit's filling and empowerment for holiness and witness.

Key texts:

- 1 Corinthians 12:13
- Ephesians 5:18

### Divine Healing

Clarify that the church prays expectantly for healing and trusts God's providence, but does not promise physical healing in the atonement in the same way forgiveness of sin is promised to all who believe.

Key texts:

- James 5:14-16
- 2 Corinthians 12:7-10

### End Times

Clarify that the church affirms Christ's visible return, resurrection, final judgment, and new creation. Members may differ on millennial and rapture details without division, while remaining submissive to Scripture and the church's teaching.

### Doctrines of Grace

State that the church affirms God's sovereign grace in salvation, including election, effectual calling, justification, sanctification, and perseverance, while teaching these truths pastorally and evangelistically.

## Discussion Questions

- In your own words, what is the gospel?
- What part of the church's mission and vision stands out to you most?
- What questions do you have about our doctrine or distinctives?

# Session 2: Our Discipleship Pathway and Ministry Strategy

## 1. Discipleship Pathway

Keep the existing four-part pathway, but make each step practical.

### Know Christ

Conversion through gospel proclamation, personal witness, prayer, and the Spirit's work.

### Join the Church

Believers are baptized and received into membership so they can be shepherded, strengthened, and accountable.

### Grow in Grace

Growth through the Word, prayer, ordinances, fellowship, service, and mutual encouragement.

### Serve and Send

Every member is equipped to serve the body and live on mission in daily life.

## 2. Ordinary Means of Grace

Explain simply:

- The Word
- Prayer
- Baptism and the Lord's Supper
- Fellowship and mutual care

## 3. Reaching the Unchurched in Carroll County

Keep the current emphasis, but connect outreach directly to membership.

Members help the church reach the unchurched by:

- Practicing hospitality.
- Inviting neighbors and family.
- Sharing the gospel in ordinary conversations.
- Following up with guests.
- Serving in mercy and recovery ministries.

## 4. Recovery and Restoration

Keep this section, but make clear that recovery ministry is part of the church's discipleship work, not a separate program detached from the church.

Include:

- Open Door or recovery gathering.
- Care team or trained mentors.
- Connection to the Lord's Day gathering.
- Pastoral care and accountability.
- Baptism and membership when credible faith and stability are present.
- Service and reintegration into the body.

## 5. Youth and Family Discipleship

Keep the existing emphasis:

- Parents as primary disciplers.
- Church as reinforcing family.
- Safe and joyful youth environment.
- Mentoring relationships.
- Clear teaching on gospel, doctrine, holiness, and mission.

## Discussion Questions

- Where do you currently see yourself in the discipleship pathway?
- Who has helped you grow spiritually?
- How can members help the church reach the unchurched without becoming program-driven?

# Session 3: Church Membership and Congregational Life

## 1. What Is the Church?

Define the church as a covenant community of baptized believers who gather under Christ's Word, live as a family, and carry out His mission.

Include the three marks already in the manual:

- Oneness: unity and visible love.
- Holiness: purity, repentance, and obedience.
- Truth: guarding and proclaiming the gospel.

## 2. Why Membership Matters

Explain membership as mutual recognition and responsibility.

Suggested wording:

Membership is the church's way of saying, "These are the people we are responsible to shepherd," and the member's way of saying, "This is my church family, and these are my leaders."

## 3. Who May Become a Member?

Keep the current requirements, but make them clear and pastoral:

- A credible profession of faith in Jesus Christ.
- Believer's baptism by immersion, or willingness to pursue baptism.
- Agreement not to oppose the church's doctrine, worship, mission, and leadership.
- Willingness to live under biblical shepherding and accountability.
- Willingness to sign the Membership Covenant.

## 4. Who May Need More Time Before Joining?

Add this new section.

A person may need more time before joining if:

- They are still unsure about the gospel or their own profession of faith.
- They have not been baptized as a believer and are not yet willing to pursue baptism.
- They disagree with the church's doctrine in a way that would create division.
- They are unwilling to live under biblical shepherding and accountability.
- They are unresolved in serious conflict or discipline at another church.
- They are mainly seeking programs, influence, or preference rather than covenant commitment.

## 5. How Members Are Received

Keep the current process, but add practical explanation.

1. Attend Abundant Life 101.
2. Complete a membership conversation with an elder/pastor.
3. Share testimony and baptism history.
4. Ask questions about doctrine, church life, or expectations.
5. If needed, schedule believer's baptism.
6. Read and sign the Membership Covenant.
7. Be recommended by the elder(s).
8. Be received by the congregation at a members meeting.

## 6. What to Expect in the Membership Conversation

Add a practical section.

The conversation should cover:

- Your testimony of conversion.
- Your baptism.
- Your church background.
- Your understanding of the gospel.
- Any major doctrinal questions.
- Any areas where you desire shepherding, care, or discipleship.
- Possible areas of service.

## 7. Duties and Privileges of Members

Divide into two balanced subsections.

### Members Commit To:

- Gather regularly.
- Participate reverently in the ordinances.
- Pray for the church.
- Pursue holiness and repentance.
- Refuse gossip and pursue reconciliation.
- Serve with their gifts.
- Give regularly, proportionally, and cheerfully as the Lord enables.
- Live on mission.
- Support biblical leadership.

### Members Can Expect the Church To:

- Preach and teach Scripture faithfully.
- Pray for them and shepherd them.
- Administer baptism and the Lord's Supper with care.
- Help them pursue holiness and restoration.
- Provide accountable leadership.
- Treat sensitive matters with wisdom, privacy, and love.
- Equip them to serve and live on mission.

## 8. Membership Categories

Revise the current categories for clarity.

### Active Member

A member who normally gathers with the church on the Lord's Day, participates in the ordinances, remains in pastoral communication, contributes to the life and mission of the church as able, and seeks to live consistently with the Membership Covenant.

### Inactive Member

A member who has been absent from the gathered life of the church for an extended period, normally 3-6 months, without meaningful pastoral communication or providential reason.

### Member Under Pastoral Care

A member receiving focused shepherding due to hardship, conflict, spiritual danger, or restoration needs, with privacy and dignity.

### Removed or Transferred Member

A person removed through transfer to another faithful church, resignation, relocation, or, if necessary, church discipline.

## 9. Membership Care Pathway

Add this new section.

When a member becomes absent, spiritually endangered, or difficult to contact, the church normally responds with:

1. Informal check-in from a pastor, elder, deacon, or member care team.
2. Pastoral conversation to understand the situation.
3. Encouragement, prayer, and a clear next step.
4. Focused pastoral care or restoration plan if needed.
5. Continued follow-up with patience and clarity.
6. If unresponsive or unrepentant, possible action by the church according to Scripture and the Membership Covenant.

## 10. Church Discipline and Restoration

Keep the current section, but add a warmer introduction.

Suggested wording:

Most discipline in the church is informal and ordinary: members encouraging, correcting, forgiving, and restoring one another in love. Formal discipline is rare and reserved for serious, outward, and unrepentant sin.

Then include:

- Purposes of discipline.
- Matthew 18 process.
- 1 Corinthians 5 seriousness.
- Galatians 6 restoration.
- Abuse, criminal activity, or immediate danger requires protective action and civil authority involvement.

## Discussion Questions

- Why does meaningful membership matter?
- What part of the Membership Covenant seems most needed in our church right now?
- How can members help one another pursue unity, holiness, and truth?

# Session 4: Leadership, Worship, and Safeguards

This could either remain part of Session 3 or become a separate fourth session. A fourth session may make the manual easier to teach without rushing.

## 1. Christ the Head of the Church

Begin with Christ's authority over the church.

## 2. Elders / Pastors / Overseers

Explain:

- Biblical office.
- Qualifications.
- Responsibilities.
- Current structure.
- Goal of a plurality of qualified elders as the Lord provides.

Avoid outdated church size language. Instead of saying "in a church of about 75 people," say:

"In a church our size, a healthy goal is a plurality of elders with a team of deacons who strengthen ministry and care."

## 3. Deacons

Explain:

- Biblical office.
- Qualifications.
- Servant-leadership role.
- How deacons support unity and practical ministry.

Possible deacon lanes:

- Mercy and Recovery Care
- Facilities and Property
- Finance and Stewardship
- Hospitality and Member Care

## 4. Ministry Teams

Clarify that ministry teams serve under pastoral oversight and do not replace biblical offices.

Include:

- Worship Ministry Team
- Oneness / Member Care Team
- Recovery and Restoration Team
- Youth Discipleship Team
- Hospitality / Guest Follow-Up Team, if desired

## 5. Congregational Life and Decision-Making

Explain elder-led, congregationally accountable church life.

Matters normally brought to the congregation:

- Receiving and removing members.
- Affirming elders and deacons.
- Approving annual budget and major non-budgeted expenditures.
- Major direction changes, property matters, mergers, or significant ministry expansions.

## 6. Members Meetings

Clarify:

- At least quarterly if possible.
- Prayer, updates, member care, receiving/removing members, stewardship decisions.
- Aim for unity, clarity, and Scripture-governed decisions.

## 7. Gathered Worship

Keep the current section with slight tightening.

Principles:

- God-centered.
- Scripture-governed.
- Congregational.
- Reverent and joyful.

Elements:

- Scripture reading.
- Expository preaching.
- Prayer.
- Congregational singing.
- Baptism and Lord's Supper.
- Giving.

## 8. Practical Policies and Safeguards

Keep this brief in the membership manual and point to separate policies.

Include:

- Child and youth safety.
- Financial integrity.
- Teaching accountability.
- Confidentiality and pastoral care.
- Mandatory reporting and civil authority involvement when required.

Note:

The Temporary Shelter Policy should remain a separate policy document, not part of the membership manual, unless formally revised and adopted by the church.

## Discussion Questions

- Why does leadership structure matter for the health of the church?
- How should members think about congregational responsibility?
- What does Scripture-shaped worship look like in practice?

- Phone
